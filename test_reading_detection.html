Clock Tree Synthesis is a critical step in physical design flow. The primary goal is to distribute the clock signal to all sequential elements with minimal skew. CTS involves inserting buffers and inverters to balance the clock tree branches. The tool analyzes the clock network and creates a balanced tree structure. Common issues include high skew between branches, excessive insertion delay, and duty cycle distortion. After CTS, the designer must verify that both setup and hold timing constraints are met across all corners. Hold violations are particularly common after CTS because the clock network delay changes the timing relationship between sequential elements. The designer must also consider on-chip variation effects on the clock tree performance.
